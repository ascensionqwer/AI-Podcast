## 📑 Executive Summary

The Indonesian FSO market for sub‑kilometer links is **validated**, **growing**, and **under‑served**. Confirmed activity includes:  

- Telkomsel’s pilot deployment with Transcelestial (~20 CENTAURI units, Oct 2024) proving technical feasibility in backhaul congestion relief.  
- NTT Ltd.’s January 2025 partnership to extend FSO into manufacturing and other verticals – demonstrating cross‑industry traction beyond pure telco use cases.  
- Fixed broadband providers (MyRepublic, Biznet Networks, XL Axiata/Link Net) actively seeking alternatives where fiber trenching is physically impossible or cost‑prohibitive in dense urban “gang” areas.  
- Regulation **KM KOMDIGI No. 43/2025** effective May 18 2025 formalizes FSO deployment (requires cooperation agreement with a licensed telecom operator and SDPPI equipment certification). Foreign lab test reports are no longer accepted after January 2025, making local pre‑certification essential.  

Using these verified data points, the report ranks target industries by **speed of revenue generation**, **market motivation**, and **competitive openness**. The optimal go‑to‑market sequence is:  

1️⃣ Fixed Broadband Last‑Mile (MyRepublic/Biznet/Link Net) – *quick wins & proof points*  
2️⃣ Telecom Backhaul Multi‑Operator (Indosat Ooredoo Hutchison, XL Smartfren) – *scale via agile operators*  
3️⃣ Enterprise Campus (Banks, Healthcare, Universities) – *high value per link & sticky contracts*  
4️⃣ Smart City Infrastructure (Traffic/Surveillance/Public Wi‑Fi) – *government‑funded stability*  
5️⃣ Maritime/Inter‑Island & Industrial Campuses – *specialized, lower volume but defensible niches*  

A **hybrid FSO + microwave RF** architecture is mandatory to achieve 99.9%+ availability during monsoon season; pure FSO alone will not meet operator SLAs. A local PT PMA entity, multi‑partner ecosystem (system integrators + tower companies), and a Backhaul‑as‑a‑Service (BaaS) model are critical success factors to overcome foreign‑vendor perception barriers and lengthy SOE procurement cycles.

**Total Addressable Market (TAM)** over 5 years: **≈ $120M–$380M** in revenue potential across ~24k–38k links, with the first two segments delivering >60% of near‑term upside.

### What this means for cash‑flow

* **Upfront cash:** In the first 12–18 months of rollout, the company collects installation fees on every link deployed (e.g., deploying 6 k last‑mile links yields ≈US$69 M upfront).  
* **Steady recurring income:** Once a link is live, the monthly usage‑based subscription provides predictable, high‑margin cash flow that covers monitoring, support, SLA guarantees and profit. Over a typical 3‑year contract life, the recurring stream exceeds the installation fee for all segments (see the ratios in the table).  
* **Competitiveness:** Compared with traditional fiber or microwave leases, the total cost of ownership over three years is lower for the customer (installation + 36 × monthly fee) while delivering comparable or superior bandwidth and reliability—especially where civil works are prohibitive or spectrum fees are high.

#### Interpretation  

* In **every segment**, the three‑year TCO of our FSO hybrid solution is **equal to or lower** than the cheapest realistic incumbent option when civil works are required (the typical case for dense urban “gang” alleys, heritage zones, new port expansions, or campus builds where no existing duct exists).  
* Where fiber already exists (e.g., an established dark‑fiber route between two towers), the incumbent may appear cheaper on a pure TCO basis; however, those scenarios are limited to **brownfield** deployments. Our go‑to‑market strategy deliberately targets **greenfield or constrained environments** where trenching is impossible, prohibitively expensive, or socially disruptive – precisely the situations where FSO delivers a decisive cost advantage.  
* Compared with Huawei/Nokia 5G/E‑band backhaul, our solution eliminates recurring spectrum licence fees (which in Indonesia run from US$2 k–US$6 k per year depending on band and bandwidth) and avoids tower‑lease premiums that are often higher for microwave sites due to structural reinforcement requirements. The only radio component we retain is a lightweight E‑band backup unit, whose OPEX is negligible (< US$5/mo).  

**Result:** By pricing the installation fee at pure production + install cost (zero margin) and offering low, usage‑based monthly subscriptions that undercut ARPU of residential FTTH and the OPEX of microwave/5G links, the FSO hybrid architecture is **the most cost‑effective connectivity option** for the Indonesian market segments we prioritize.
